# MC Script - Dave Muir
## Clinical AI Hackathon - City St George's, University of London
## Friday 20 March 2026

---

## Opening Ceremony - HG01 Birley, Centenary Building - 09:00

- Welcome everyone to the Clinical AI Hackathon.
- Thank you for being here, and thank you for the work you have put in this week.
- We have 74 participants joining us across the hackathon.
- The challenge has been to turn MDT Word documents into structured data.
- This is a real clinical problem: less manual admin, more usable data, and better support for patient care and research.
- The problem was brought to us by Dr Anita Wale.
- The synthetic dataset was curated by Ellie Hickey.
- Thank you as well to Dr Alex Nicholls and Hitesh Patel for supporting the event and judging.
- Our judging panel today is Dr Anita Wale, Dr Alex Nicholls, and Hitesh Patel.
- Over the week, teams worked through workshops on AI agents, LLM APIs, and applied clinical data problems.
- That work has led to 7 final team entries presenting this morning.

### Questions to the audience

- Quick show of hands: who has worked with clinical data before this week?
- And who has built with LLMs or AI agents for the first time during this hackathon?

### Before presentations begin

- A few quick reminders before we start.
- Please keep side conversations to a minimum during presentations.
- Judges will score each team after they present.
- We will keep tightly to time so every team gets a fair slot.
- Please give every team your full attention and a good round of applause.
- Teams, please make sure your slides or demos are ready before your slot.

### Transition to presentations

- We are looking forward to seeing what you have built.
- Good luck to all teams.
- Please make your way to your allocated presentation room.

---

## Closing Ceremony - Tait Building C322 - 14:00

- Welcome back everyone.
- Thank you for a strong set of presentations this morning.
- We have seen practical, creative, and thoughtful responses to a real NHS challenge.
- Several teams showed not just technical skill, but a clear understanding of clinical context.
- Thank you to our judges for your time, expertise, and careful scoring.
- Thank you to our NHS and university partners, and to everyone who helped run the week.

### Questions to the audience

- Before the awards, one quick question: who is leaving today with a new idea they want to keep building?
- And who has met someone this week they want to collaborate with again?

### Awards setup

- We are now moving to the awards.
- As I announce each category, please hold your applause until the winner has been named.
- After each winner is announced, we will invite them forward for their prize and a photo.
